About Me

I'm a graduate student working on my doctoral degree in the Department of Mechanical Aerospace and Nuclear Engineering (MANE) at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI), Troy NY. My doctoral research is focused on developing an implicit block-structured mesh library (pumiMBBL) for large-scale simulations of Plasma-Material interactions. Over the last three years at RPI, I have also extensively researched on developing adaptive numerical methods for stochastic PDEs using multi-level/multi-fidelity uncertainty quantification (UQ) methods. Other research topics that I have worked on over the years and still interested in includes stabilized finite-element models for advection-dominated flows, high-performance parallel computing and applied math.

Research

My passion for applied mathematics and programming has been a key motivation for all the research projects that I have been involved during my time at IIT, Madras and at RPI. My current reserach interests includes mesh development for large-scale plasma simulations, high-performance computing, uncertainty quantification (both forward and backward propagation) with focus on multi-level/multi-fidelity models, mesh adaptation based on local discretization error and stabilzed finite-element based solvers for advection-dominated fluid flow problems.
